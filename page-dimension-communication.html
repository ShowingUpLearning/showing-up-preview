Great Copywriting Is Great Consultancy Practice

In the pace of consulting, writing is often rushed or overlooked. Yet clear, thoughtful written communication still sets great consultants apart, especially in the age of AI. This module shares practical ways to strengthen your writing and elevate your professional impact.

Editing Is Overlooked (And It Can Cost You)

Consulting moves fast, and editing is often the first discipline to disappear. Yet careful review and external perspective can be the difference between an average proposal and an exceptional one. This module explores why rigorous editing elevates credibility and commercial impact.

Summarise Your Ideas Into One Clear Sentence

The best communicators make the complex feel simple, and the simple feel compelling. In this short, sharp module, you will learn how to refine your thinking by focusing on the right verbs and precise nouns to express your ideas in one clear sentence that people remember.

The Principles and Pillars of Compelling Presentations

This opening module sets the foundations for writing presentations that truly land. You will explore the core principles and structural pillars that make ideas clear, persuasive, and memorable before moving on to practical tools and techniques.

Step 1: Begin With the End in Mind in Every Presentation

Knowing how you want your audience to feel and what action you want them to take is the cornerstone of every strong presentation. Before writing a single slide, define the decision you are guiding and the shift you want to create so your message lands with purpose.

Step 2: Four Ways to Authentically Engage an Audience

Introducing yourself or a new idea can feel routine, yet the approach you choose makes a difference. This module explores four distinct ways to open meetings and engage a room, so you can begin in a manner that feels authentic and genuinely engaging.

Step 3: Using Authentic Anecdotes to Engage Audiences

Stories make ideas memorable. This step explores how to use authentic anecdotes to humanise your message, build credibility and create a connection. When grounded in real experience, your presentation feels genuine, and your audience is far more likely to engage.

Step 4: Celebrating Strengths Before the Journey Ahead

You are about to take your client on a hero's journey, and every journey begins with honouring the past. This step shows how to celebrate what is already great, recognise progress made and set a tone of respect and belief before inviting them into what comes next.

Step 5: The Challenge Ahead and the Case for Change

Every journey requires a challenge worth rising to. This step explores how to clearly and respectfully define the real issue, creating urgency without drama. When the challenge is framed well, your audience sees why change matters and feels ready to move forward.

Step 6: The Opportunity Before Us and What Is Possible

Once the challenge is clear, the opportunity becomes visible. This step explores how to paint a compelling picture of what could be achieved, in practical and human terms. When the future feels tangible and worthwhile, your audience feels inspired to see the path forward.

Step 7: What We Need to Do, Clearly and Compellingly

This is where vision turns practical. In this step, you outline the key actions required in a way that is structured, focused and persuasive (with integrity). Clear ideas, presented with energy and intent, give your audience confidence in the direction to move forward.

Step 8: Why We Need to Do It, Grounded in Evidence

This step is rooted in data, insight and perspective. You will learn how to separate signal from noise, focus on what truly matters and present evidence in a way that strengthens your case. When the rationale is clear and credible, decisions feel confident and well-founded.

Step 9: How We Do It and What It Looks Like in Action

This is where strategy becomes tangible. In this step, you explain how the work will unfold, what it will look like in practice and how progress will be measured. Clear delivery builds confidence and reassures your audience that the path forward is realistic and achievable.

Step 10: What It Means for You and the Part You Play

This step connects the path ahead to what it means for each person and team. You make it clear why their role matters and how they can contribute. When people see themselves in the story, commitment grows, and the vision becomes something they choose to be part of.

Step 11: The Obstacles Before Us and How We Navigate Them

Every meaningful change brings resistance and risk. This step addresses the obstacles openly and without drama, separating real concerns from imagined ones. By acknowledging challenges clearly, you build credibility and prepare your audience to move forward with resilience.

Step 12: The Vision of Where We'll Be When We Succeed

This final step paints a clear and compelling picture of success. You describe what the future will feel like, how things will improve and what will be different as a result of the work. When the destination feels tangible, people leave energised and ready to act.
