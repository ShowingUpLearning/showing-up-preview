1 Define

What great curiosity looks like

There are three distinct ways of being curious, and the difference between them matters enormously. Epistemic curiosity digs deep into facts, figures and the analysis underneath the data. Diversive curiosity wanders sideways, collecting dots long before you can see how they will join up. Empathetic curiosity takes a sincere interest in the people the work will affect, right to the far end of the change.

Most of us have a natural spike in one. The best nurture all three, and that is how you ask the most thoughtful questions in the room.
